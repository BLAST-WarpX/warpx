# Dielectric Wall Capability Assessment — WarpX Hybrid Ohm's-Law Solver

## 1. Executive Summary

**No — WarpX cannot model a dielectric embedded-boundary (EB) wall today, in any solver path.** Every EB wall in WarpX is a perfect electric conductor (PEC): the EM/FDTD path freezes cut-cell field components via `eb_update_E`/`eb_update_B` masks, the electrostatic path supports only `setEBDirichlet` (fixed-potential conductor), and the hybrid Ohm's-law path applies PEC mirror/fold fills (`ApplyPECBoundaryToField`) that force tangential E to zero. There is **no BC-type selector for the EB**, no relative permittivity (`eps_r`) attached to EB geometry, no surface/bound-charge tracking, and no normal-D continuity condition anywhere. Two ingredients exist but are decoupled from what's needed: (a) a full volumetric macroscopic (`epsilon`/`mu`/`sigma`) FDTD medium that is not coupled to EB geometry and not usable in the hybrid solver, and (b) AMReX's variable-coefficient `MLEBNodeFDLaplacian` (which *could* express `div(eps grad phi)`), already called by WarpX — but only for a plasma-dressing coefficient, never for permittivity, and never by the hybrid solver (which is not potential-based). One dielectric-relevant piece is genuinely present: the **electron-pressure Neumann BC** (`ApplyEBBoundaryToNodalScalar(Pe, odd=false)`) is already ~80% of a plasma-confining pressure condition. The E-field-interface half is greenfield. Feature keys: `eb-wall-bc-types`, `electrostatic-dielectric-eps`, `hybrid-eb-E-bc`, `pressure-density-eb-bc`, `macroscopic-eps-sigma`, `particle-wall-bc`.

## 3. Electric-Field Effects at the Dielectric Interface

### What the physics requires
At a dielectric–plasma interface with permittivities `eps1` (plasma/vacuum side) and `eps2 = eps_r·eps0` (wall side):
- **Tangential E continuity:** `E1_t = E2_t` (the tangential field is *continuous*, generally nonzero).
- **Normal D continuity with surface charge:** `eps1·E1_n − eps2·E2_n = sigma_s`, where `sigma_s` is the free surface charge; with no free charge this reduces to `eps1·E1_n = eps2·E2_n`.
- **Surface-charge accumulation:** a `sigma_s` field on the interface that evolves from the impinging normal current/flux (charged particles landing on an insulator do not conduct away).

### What WarpX does at the wall today
The EB is a PEC in all three paths, i.e. `E_t = 0` on the surface and no permittivity anywhere:

- **EM/FDTD path:** `MarkUpdateCellsStairCase` (`EmbeddedBoundaryInit.cpp:129`) turns cut cells into `eb_update_E`/`eb_update_B` masks; the update kernels multiply by these masks so covered/cut components are frozen → emergent PEC. Even under `algo.em_solver_medium=macroscopic`, the covered E is simply *not updated* (`MacroscopicEvolveE.cpp:178`), and the only interface treatment is a cell-centered `epsilon` interpolated to Yee edges (`:181-185`) — a smeared staircase permittivity, first-order at best, **not** a D-continuity condition (`macroscopic-eps-sigma`).
- **Electrostatic path:** the EB enters `MLEBNodeFDLaplacian`, and the *only* EB API invoked is `setEBDirichlet(phi_EB)` (`PoissonSolver.H:364-366`) — a fixed-potential conductor. `rho` is pre-scaled by the scalar vacuum `epsilon_0` (`PoissonSolver.H:288`) and the scalar `setSigma` carries only beam anisotropy. No Robin/jump EB coefficient is called, so `eps1·E1_n = eps2·E2_n` cannot be expressed (`electrostatic-dielectric-eps`).
- **Hybrid Ohm's-law path:** E is *algebraic* (`E = (enE − grad Pe)/max(rho, rho_floor)`, `HybridPICSolveE.cpp:1497-1507`), recomputed each substep, so the wall BC is re-imposed by `ApplyPECBoundaryToField` on E every substep (`HybridPICModel.cpp:926`). The tangential weight `w_t = s/d_im < 0` forces `E_t → 0` at the wall (odd parity), and the normal weight `w_n = 1` gives zero normal gradient on `E_n` (`EBJBoundary.cpp:1354-1356`, `mirror_combine` `:710`) — this is precisely the PEC limit, the *opposite* of the dielectric tangential-continuity requirement, and the normal treatment is zero-gradient not eps-weighted D continuity (`hybrid-eb-E-bc`).

### The gap, path by path
- **ES path (best-positioned):** the AMReX building block is already present and exercised — `MLEBNodeFDLaplacian::setSigma(lev, MultiFab)` → `mlebndfdlap_sig_adotx` implements genuine `div(sigma grad phi)` with face-averaged `sigma` and cut-cell volume weighting (`AMReX_MLEBNodeFDLap_2D_K.H:426-443`, EB variant `:472-530`), which *natively honors normal-flux continuity across a coefficient jump*. To repurpose for a dielectric: build an `epsilon` MultiFab, pass it as the Poisson `sigma`, and stop pre-dividing `rho` by a global `eps0` (fold `eps` into the operator). This handles a **mesh-region eps jump**; a cut-cell EB with dielectric *behind* it (covered region carrying `eps_r` + a non-Dirichlet EB BC) is a further step (`electrostatic-dielectric-eps`).
- **Hybrid path (greenfield):** no Poisson/`phi` at all — E is algebraic, so a Poisson-based dielectric BC does not plug in. A dielectric E-interface must be imposed *as a field BC in the Ohm's-law E fill*: replace the PEC tangential-odd parity (`w_t=s/d_im`) with tangential continuity, add an `eps_r`-weighted normal-D jump on `E_n`, and add a `sigma_s` accumulator fed by normal current at the wall. None of this exists (`hybrid-eb-E-bc`, `pressure-density-eb-bc`).

## 4. Electron-Pressure Neumann Condition

The confining term in the generalized Ohm's law is `−grad(Pe)/(n·q_e)`, evaluated per edge as `grad_Pe = T_Algo::UpwardDx(Pe,...)` — a plain 2nd-order FD stencil, **not** EB-aware (`HybridPICSolveE.cpp:1490-1507`). The wall condition is imposed entirely through mirror-filled ghost values, not a modified/one-sided operator.

### What exists today
- **Pe is already Neumann at the EB.** `CalculateElectronPressure` calls `ApplyEBBoundaryToNodalScalar(Pe, ..., /*odd=*/false)` hard-coded (`HybridPICModel.cpp:968`), an even (zero-normal-gradient) reflection, with the in-code rationale *"the conducting wall supports the plasma back-pressure."* The write is `f = odd ? (s/d_im)*f_im : f_im`; the even branch copies the fluid image verbatim (`EBJBoundary.cpp:2241`). Pe itself comes from a polytropic EOS `n0·T0·(n/n0)^gamma` on `max(rho,0)` (`HybridPICModel.H:756`, `HybridPICModel.cpp:1009`).
- **rho parity is switchable** via `m_eb_rho_dirichlet` (default true = Dirichlet-0; can be set false → Neumann) with a deposit fold (`FoldEBDepositToNodalScalar`, parity via `eb_deposit_fold` = `pec`/`reflect`) so wall-adjacent charge is conserved (`WarpXPushFieldsHybridPIC.cpp:388-397`, `HybridPICModel.cpp:258-268`).

### Does it confine the plasma?
Partly, yes. With Pe even-reflected, a `grad(Pe)` stencil straddling the wall sees ~zero normal pressure gradient at the surface, so the `−grad(Pe)` term does not push flux normally through the wall. Combined with `eb_deposit_fold='reflect'` and `eb_rho_dirichlet=false` (rho Neumann), a pressure-supported column can be held against the wall as a **runtime configuration, not new code** — this is ~80% of a plasma-confining pressure BC (`pressure-density-eb-bc`).

### Caveats and what a proper pressure-Neumann needs
- The condition is only as accurate as the 1-cell mirror band (`d_band = h_max`); the `grad(Pe)` operator is not EB-aware (no one-sided differencing).
- **At partially-covered cut edges the entire Hall+pressure term is dropped** (`E = eta·J`, `partial_resistive_only`, `HybridPICSolveE.cpp:1490-1493`), so the confining `−grad(Pe)` is silently absent exactly on the cells adjacent to the wall.
- Pe parity is hard-coded (`odd=false`) with no flag.
- There is **no sheath / floating-potential / surface-charge feedback** linking confined pressure to the wall E-field — the real mechanism by which a dielectric charges and confines.
- A proper zero-normal-flux pressure-Neumann would (a) expose the Pe parity as a flag, (b) keep the pressure term active on cut cells via an EB-aware one-sided `grad(Pe)`, and (c) couple the pressure/flux condition to `sigma_s` and `E_n` rather than a bare mirror fill. It hooks into `HybridPICModel::CalculateElectronPressure` (`:958-979`) and the E-solve pressure stencil (`HybridPICSolveE.cpp:1490-1507`).

## 5. Particle Handling

EB particle handling is **absorb-only** (`particle-wall-bc`). `scrapeParticlesAtEB` (`ParticleScraper.H:66-223`) applies a user functor when a particle crosses within `offset` of the level set, but every production call site hard-codes `ParticleBoundaryProcess::Absorb()` (`MultiParticleContainer.cpp:1243`, `WarpXParticleContainer.cpp:350`, `AddParticles.cpp:1223/1759`), which just invalidates the particle id. Only `NoOp` and `Absorb` functors exist (`ParticleBoundaryProcess.H:18-37`); there is **no reflecting or thermal EB functor**.

The `eb_particle_scrape_offset` **standoff** is fully working (`WarpX.cpp:807`, `WarpXEvolve.cpp:755`, gate at `ParticleScraper.H:195`) but only moves the *absorbing* surface inward — it controls *where* the wall acts, not *whether* it absorbs. It is orthogonal to (and composes with) a future reflecting functor.

**What a non-absorbing dielectric needs:** a real dielectric must not dump plasma through the wall — particle flux must be confined, consistent with the pressure-Neumann. The scraper is already the right hook (it hands the functor the exact collision `pos`, unit wall `normal` via `DistanceToEB::interp_normal`, and an RNG at `ParticleScraper.H:201-217`). What is missing:
- A **specular EB reflector** (reflect velocity about `normal`, mirror position back across the wall via signed distance) and/or a **diffuse/thermal EB re-emitter** (half-Maxwellian along `normal`, full-Maxwellian tangential). The physics already exists for *axis-aligned domain walls* (`ParticleBoundaries_K.H:38-89`) and must be generalized to the arbitrary EB normal and packaged as a new `ParticleBoundaryProcess` functor.
- **Plumbing to select the EB particle BC** — no `warpx.eb_particle_boundary` (or per-species) parameter analogous to the domain `ParticleBoundaryType` (`WarpXAlgorithmSelection.H:144-151`).
- **User input for an EB reflection model** — `reflection_model_eb` was scaffolded in PICMI but is **commented out** (`picmi.py:269,383`) with no C++ parse (unlike the six domain `reflection_model_*` parsers, `ParticleBoundaries.H:60-89`).
- The `save_particles_at_eb` / `ParticleBoundaryBuffer` accounting (`ParticleBoundaryBuffer.cpp:504,131`) assumes lost/absorbed particles and would need revisiting for a non-absorbing EB.

## 6. Gaps & Recommended Implementation Path

Ordered small-to-large. Each item notes the target files and whether it is a quick win or larger work.

### Quick wins (config / small code, low risk)
1. **Expose the Pe EB parity as a flag.** Pe Neumann is hard-coded `odd=false` (`HybridPICModel.cpp:968`); add `hybrid_pic_model.eb_pe_dirichlet` (default false=Neumann) mirroring `m_eb_rho_dirichlet`. Lets users toggle the pressure BC without recompiling. *(pressure-density-eb-bc)*
2. **Document + validate the existing confining config.** `eb_deposit_fold='reflect'` + `eb_rho_dirichlet=false` + Pe-Neumann already gives a pressure-confined column; add a dedicated confinement test (the current EB tests cover PEC image-current / B-push / eb_diffusion, not this combination) and expose the combo cleanly in PICMI. *(pressure-density-eb-bc)*
3. **Add a specular/diffuse EB particle functor + selector.** Write `ReflectSpecular`/`ReflectThermal` `ParticleBoundaryProcess` functors generalizing `ParticleBoundaries_K.H:38-89` to the EB `normal`; add a `warpx.eb_particle_boundary` (`absorb`/`reflect`/`thermal`) parameter and replace the hard-coded `Absorb()` at the four call sites. Un-comment and wire `reflection_model_eb` (`picmi.py:269,383`). This is the cheapest way to stop the wall from dumping plasma and is largely self-contained. *(particle-wall-bc)*

### Medium (new field-BC coefficient, contained to one solver)
4. **Attach a permittivity field to the wall region.** Reuse the `MacroscopicProperties` `epsilon(x,y,z)` parser/MultiFab machinery (`MacroscopicProperties.H:69,101`) — currently FDTD-only — to build an `eps_r` MultiFab tagged on the near-wall / covered cells. This is the shared prerequisite for every field-side dielectric effect. *(eb-wall-bc-types, electrostatic-dielectric-eps)*
5. **Dielectric ES Poisson (mesh-region eps jump).** Generalize `ablastr::fields::computePhi` to (a) accept an `epsilon` MultiFab, (b) call the *variable-coefficient* `MLEBNodeFDLaplacian::setSigma(lev, eps_mf)` (already exercised via `EffectivePotentialPoissonSolver.H:183`), and (c) remove/relocate the vacuum-`eps0` pre-scaling of `rho` (`PoissonSolver.H:288`). This yields correct `eps1·E1_n = eps2·E2_n` continuity for a dielectric *region* essentially for free at the AMReX level. Note: this benefits the ES path only, **not** the hybrid solver. *(electrostatic-dielectric-eps)*
6. **Add a free-surface-charge source path.** Provide a `sigma_s` field addable as an interface node source in the ES solve, and/or accumulate scraped-particle charge onto interface nodes. Today `ChargeOnEB` only *diagnoses* induced conductor charge (`ChargeOnEB.cpp:189-216`); it does not feed back a BC. *(electrostatic-dielectric-eps)*

### Larger (the hybrid E-interface — greenfield)
7. **Add an EB BC-type selector for fields.** There is no enum/parameter to choose EB behavior (currently hard-coded PEC via masking/Dirichlet). Introduce an EB field-BC selector (`warpx.eb_field_boundary` = `pec`/`dielectric`) as the dispatch point for items 8–10. *(eb-wall-bc-types)*
8. **Dielectric parity in the hybrid EB E fill.** In `ApplyPECBoundaryToField` / `mirror_combine` (`EBJBoundary.cpp:710,1354-1356`), replace the PEC tangential-odd weight `w_t=s/d_im` with **tangential continuity** and the normal zero-gradient `w_n=1` with an **`eps_r`-weighted normal-D jump** on `E_n`, gated by the new selector. This is the core of the E-interface effect and has no existing analogue. *(hybrid-eb-E-bc)*
9. **Surface-charge accumulator in the hybrid path.** Track `sigma_s = eps0·(E_n^plasma − eps_r·E_n^wall)`, evolved from the impinging normal ion current at the wall (instead of zeroing the deep interior at `EBJBoundary.cpp:1766`). Couple it into the `E_n` jump of item 8 and, for closure, into the pressure/flux condition of item 11. *(hybrid-eb-E-bc, pressure-density-eb-bc)*
10. **Revisit rho parity for a dielectric.** On an insulator, wall charge should *accumulate*, not be Dirichlet-zeroed or mirror-reflected. Extend the rho handling (`WarpXPushFieldsHybridPIC.cpp:397`) with an accumulating option tied to `sigma_s`. *(hybrid-eb-E-bc)*
11. **EB-aware, always-on pressure gradient at cut cells.** Keep the Hall+pressure term active on partially-covered cut edges (currently dropped to `E=eta·J`, `HybridPICSolveE.cpp:1490-1493`) using an EB-aware one-sided `grad(Pe)`, so the confining `−grad(Pe)` is not silently missing on exactly the wall-adjacent cells; couple to sheath/`sigma_s` feedback for the physical charging mechanism. *(pressure-density-eb-bc)*

### Not recommended as the primary route
- **Volumetric macroscopic `epsilon`** (`MacroscopicEvolveE`) gives only a crude staircase dielectric *block* (no D-continuity interface BC, EB coupling is PEC masking only), is Cartesian-only (RZ aborts — relevant to the cylindrical-correction branch), has no PICMI, and is **completely decoupled from the hybrid solver** (which computes E algebraically with no displacement-current term). It is not reusable for a hybrid dielectric wall without new code. *(macroscopic-eps-sigma)*

**Summary of effort split:** the electron-pressure-Neumann half is a flag-and-test exercise (~items 1–2) that is already ~80% present; a reflecting particle wall is a contained new functor (~item 3); a dielectric *region* in the ES solver is a medium AMReX-plumbing task (~items 4–6) that leverages an operator WarpX already calls; but the **E-field dielectric interface in the hybrid Ohm's-law path (items 7–11) is genuinely greenfield** — no permittivity, no surface charge, no D-continuity, and a PEC-only parity that must be replaced.
